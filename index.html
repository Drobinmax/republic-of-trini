Dr. Keith Christopher Rowley: The 7th Prime Minister of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago

November 14, 2019November 14, 2019 Beverly Ma Uncategorized

Dr. Keith Christopher Rowley was born in Mason Hall, Tobago, on October 24, 1949, and raised by his grandparents, the prominent Tobago farmers. He finished his secondary education at Bishop’s High School, (BHS) Tobago, and his excellent student performance saw him catch the highly regarded Sylvan Bowles Scholarship at BHS. He then began his studies at the University of West Indies, Mona, Jamaica, where he graduated with a BSc. Geology (First Class Honors), Geography (First Class Honors); Volcanic Stratigraphy MSc, UWI, St. Augustine.

When he was a research scientist, he held a number of positions, including Research Fellow and Head of the Seismic Research Unit at the University of West Indies, St. Augustine. In addition, he was also the General Manager of the State-owned National Quarries Company Limited.

He thought of entering the political arena and decide to run for his first candidacy in 1981 to contest the Tobago West seat. To date, Keith Rowley has the distinction of being the only candidate of the Peoples National Movement (PNM) to have won the general election in both Tobago and Trinidad. He became the Member of the House of Representatives for Diego Martin West in the Parliament of Trinidad and Tobago, where he has represented that constituency since 1991 having been re-elected in 1995, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2007 and 2010.

Following the defeat of the People’s National Movement (PNM) in the 2010 General Election of Trinidad and Tobago, Dr. Rowley was named Leader of the Opposition of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago and elected Political Leader of the People’s National Movement in 2010.

On September 7, 2015, he led the National People’s Movement to a general election victory, becoming the 7th Prime Minister of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago.
